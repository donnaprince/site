    Dart Bridge

    March 6, 2018- March 25, 2018
    Partner Project

    The main intention of this project was to create a highway, on the software West Point Bridge. Our client, American Bridge Company, had been contracted to construct a bridge crossing part of the Trinity River. The decision matrix for this project was created on a Google Sheet , in which the four prototypes created were compared with one another, using point distribution rubric. The constraint that we faced in this project was not being able to cross the budget of $300,000, but we were able to spend $298,000. To be cost-effective we used the cheapest material, carbon steel on the Hinge Arch-Warren truss template. The concept of engineering applied to this project was how to ensure the bridge would undergo the least amount of tension (blue colored members) and compression (red-colored members). To do this, members that did not undergo any pressure were then reduced in size, while members that had tension/compression were increased in size, thus reducing their tension/compression. An aspect that I contributed to this project in regards to the documentation was writing the design brief, decision matrix paragraph, annotating our final solution prototypes. In terms of creating a bridge prototype, I created two prototypes that performed well under the tension and compression the bridge underwent as a result of the truck crossing the bridge. Through this project, our team was able to produce a bridge that had very little movement indicating the ability of the bridge to withstand the heavy load. Above, is a video that demonstrates the high quality of our two-lane highway.
